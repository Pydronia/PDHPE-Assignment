45% of all young Australian injury related deaths are from road crashes.

As a learner driver, it is important to understand the road rules and be constantly aware of the situation. It can otherwise cost you your life. This campaign is aimed at learner drivers in order to educate and inform of the dangers of driving and encourage safe driving practices.

Click here for some harsh stats.

The biggest killer of young drivers is speeding.

An increase in speed by just 5km/h will result in a 15% increase in crashes. Slow down and sit below the speed limit. Speeding is never good. It only adds to the risk of crashing as it increases your stopping distance, lowers the time you have to react, and decreases your control. As a learner, aim to stay 5km/h under the speed limit. This will significantly decrease your risk of crashing and give you time to react to your situation.

Wear a seatbelt - it's just common sense.

Teens wear seatbelts to a much lesser extent than adults. It seems silly to just leave a safety precaution unused - don't you think? Make sure you and all your passengers are buckled up before you start the engine.
